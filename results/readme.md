# Summary of Key Results

This document summarizes the main findings from the global weather analysis (NOAA GSOD data, 77 selected stations, 2014–2024 period).  
All insights are derived from the visualizations generated in the project.

### 1. Precipitation Patterns (Heavy Rain Days >10 mm)

- Europe shows by far the highest average number of days with precipitation >10 mm, frequently 30–50+ days per year, with a dramatic peak of ~54 days in 2016.
- South America also experiences several very wet years (e.g. ~54 days in 2021).
- Asia and Africa show moderate values with an increasing trend toward 2024 (Asia reaching ~26 days in 2024).
- Oceania remains the driest continent, often below 5 days in recent years, with high year-to-year variability.
- North America stays relatively stable (around 20–28 days).

**Main takeaway**: Europe and parts of South America/Asia have seen the most frequent heavy rain events in the period.

### 2. Temperature Rankings in 2024

- **Continents**: Africa was the warmest continent in 2024 (~21–22 °C), followed by Asia. Europe ranked as the coolest (~14 °C).
- **Countries**: Egypt led with the highest national average (~26 °C), followed by Morocco and other warm/subtropical countries.
- **Cities**: The 10 hottest cities were dominated by tropical locations. Yola (Nigeria) topped the list at ~30 °C, followed by other stations in Nigeria, India, Indonesia, Jamaica, Egypt, El Salvador, and Mexico.

**Main takeaway**: Tropical and subtropical stations (especially in Africa and parts of Asia/South America) consistently occupy the warmest positions.

### 3. Temperature Variability (Standard Deviation)

- Asia and North America exhibit the highest year-to-year intra-annual temperature variability (~5.5–6.3 °C), indicating stronger fluctuations.
- South America shows the lowest and most stable variability (~2.5–3.0 °C) throughout the decade.
- Europe and Africa fall in the middle range.
- No clear long-term increase or decrease in variability is visible across the 2014–2024 period.

**Main takeaway**: Continental climates (Asia, North America) experience more temperature swings than tropical/subtropical regions.

### 4. Long-term Temperature Trends (2014–2024)

- Africa records the highest absolute temperatures (~20–22 °C) and shows the clearest upward tendency in recent years.
- Asia remains stable around 20–21 °C.
- North America and South America fluctuate around 17–19 °C.
- Europe is consistently the coolest (~14–15 °C), but with small increases visible in the most recent years.
- Oceania is the most stable and lowest (~17 °C).

**Main takeaway**: Africa exhibits the strongest warming signal among the continents in this decade; Europe remains coolest but is not immune to gradual increases.

### 5. Year-by-Year Top 10 Hottest Cities (2014–2024)

- Tropical cities from Nigeria, Jamaica, Indonesia, El Salvador, India, Egypt, Mexico, Brazil, and Tanzania repeatedly dominate the annual top-10 rankings.
- The warmest annual averages frequently reach 28–32 °C in the leading cities.
- The pattern is remarkably consistent across the decade — only minor shifts in order occur from year to year.

**Main takeaway**: The same group of tropical/subtropical stations consistently produces the highest annual mean temperatures.

Overall pattern (2014–2024):
- Strong tropical/subtropical dominance in highest temperatures
- Elevated frequency of heavy precipitation days in Europe and parts of South America/Asia
- Greater temperature variability in Asia and North America
- General stability or slow warming in continental averages, with Africa showing the clearest upward trend
